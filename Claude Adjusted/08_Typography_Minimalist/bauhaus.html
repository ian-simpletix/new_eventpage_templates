Join us for a day-long celebration of the Bauhaus movement's enduring influence on art, architecture, and design. This symposium brings together leading scholars, practitioners, and enthusiasts to explore the legacy of one of the most influential design schools in history.

From its founding in 1919 to its forced closure in 1933, the Bauhaus revolutionized how we think about the relationship between art, craft, and industry. Its principles continue to shape contemporary design across all disciplines.

KEY THEMES

Architecture

Exploring Bauhaus principles in contemporary building design and urban planning.

Typography

The revolution in graphic design and the lasting impact of geometric sans-serif typefaces.

Industrial Design

How Bauhaus thinking transformed product design and manufacturing.
