About The Lecture

Join us for an evening with Dr. Eleanor Vance as she discusses her decades-long research into the urban development of Rome, from its ancient foundations to the challenges of the modern era.

Drawing from her recent work in the Vatican Archives, Dr. Vance will explore how Rome's layered history continues to shape contemporary urban planning and cultural identity.

About the Speaker

Dr. Eleanor Vance is Professor of Classical History at Cambridge University and author of Seven Hills: The Making of Ancient Rome. Her work has been featured in The New York Times, National Geographic, and the BBC.
